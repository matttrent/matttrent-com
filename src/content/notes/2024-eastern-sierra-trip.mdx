### A preface about photography and documenting my travels

I'll eventually move this somewhere else 
- Documenting my trips in photo essays was the primary motivation for updating this website
- I had a photography mentorship with [Jakob Lilja-Ruiz](https://www.jakoblr.com/) (aka [portra papi](https://www.instagram.com/portra_papi)) earlier this year, and one of the topics covered was curating series of your work
- I attempted to practice this through posting series on Instagram, but I struggled with it
- I wasn't being very imaginative in my choices.  I was mostly coming up with collections of similar things like silhouettes or certain landscapes.  And the required regularity of posting that social media encourages was making it feel like a chore
- I found myself wanting a different format that put me in a more narrative frame of mind for it.  
- I regularly go on trips exploring the Western US and documenting those travels is already the focus of my photography at the moment
- Taking inspiration from [Bound for Nowhere](https://www.boundfornowhere.com/) and [Adventure Taco](https://adventuretaco.com/), I realized what I wanted to be doing is documenting the those travels.  Thinking more about series of photographs can come up in the natural progression inherent to those trips
- Entirely unoriginal but personally satisfying

The entire [site redesign](colophon) was for this purpose
- As such I'm still building the components that let me present text and photography together in the way I want
- And there will likely be some rough edges in the meantime

## The trip

- get out
- no strong itinerary
- wanted to get a least one good hike in 
- and wanted to get some good photography sessions, part of the experiment being trying out the fuji 55-140 lens that I rented and understanding how and where I could use that in the pictures I wanted to make.  
- beyond that my aspirations were modest and I was fine to go with the flow, spend more time in camp or otherwise hanging out, rather than adventure-maxing
